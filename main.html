Biogas Overview

Biogas is a renewable energy source produced by the breakdown of organic materials such as agricultural waste, food scraps, and animal manure in an anaerobic environment. It primarily consists of methane, which can be used for heating, electricity generation, and as a fuel source for vehicles.

Biogas production helps reduce waste, provides an alternative to fossil fuels, and contributes to environmental sustainability by reducing greenhouse gas emissions.
